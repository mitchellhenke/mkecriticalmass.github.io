Milwaukee Critical Mass Walkie-talkie Radio Frequencies

[Home]

Walkie-talkies can help with communication throughout the group to ensure there's a good pace, roads are safe, and that the right people know what's happening. Different manufactures and types of radios can have different ways to set channels, so here's some information that should help you get connected to the group.

  • Primary
  • Secondary
  • Fallback

Primary Channel

This is the default channel for a ride: channel 2 for the two wheels on a bike! If there's interference we might switch to the secondary channel.

Setting Value Details
Channel 2 Most walkie-talkies use this number to identify the frequency being used.
Frequency 462.5875 MHz Higher-end units will show the actual radio frequency in use.
Privacy Code Type DCS / CDCSS Continuous Digital-Coded Squelch System is an additional setting to reduce interference between groups. It does not encrypt or otherwise hide your transmissions.
CDCSS Setting by Manufacturer Different brands use different numbers to label DCS settings. Find the brand of your unit to see the setting you should use.

Fallback Channel

If the other two channels have problems, our fallback is channel 4 with no privacy codes.

Channel 4 Most walkie-talkies use this number to identify the frequency being used.
Frequency 4462.6375 MHz Higher-end units will show the actual radio frequency in use.
Privacy Code Type none
